Hamburger

Sandwich consisting of a cooked meat patty on a bun or roll

A hamburger, or simply burger, is a sandwich consisting of fillings—usually a patty of ground meat, typically beef—placed inside a sliced bun or bread roll. Hamburgers are often served with cheese, lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, bacon, or chilis; condiments such as ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise, relish, or a "special sauce," often a variation of Thousand Island dressing; and are frequently placed on sesame seed buns. A hamburger patty topped with cheese is called a cheeseburger.

Ingredients

  • Ground beef
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Buns
  • Lettuce
  • Tomato
  • Onion
  • Ketchup
  • Mayonnaise
  • Mustard
  • Sliced cheese(optional)

Steps

  1. Form ground beef into beef patty. Should be approximately a quarter of a pound.
  2. Season beef patty with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  3. Cook patty on barbecue grill or skillet on high heat.
  4. Flip after approximately 5 minutes and cook other side for an additional 5 minutes.
  5. For a cheeseburger add a slice of cheese of your choice.
  6. Dress buns with toppings and condiments.
  7. Place cooked patty on top of lower bun.
  8. Place top bun and press down to keep sandwich together.
  9. Enjoy!
